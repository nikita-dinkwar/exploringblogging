Best Foods that you MUST TASTE in LADAKH

1.Thukpa
Thukpa is basically a noodle soup with boiled veggies, chunks of chicken, pork and beef. The blend of all these mouth-watering ingredients is served with spicy chutney to enhance the overall zing of this dish. The best place to try: Chopsticks Noodle Bar, Leh.

2.Khambir Khambir is a traditional bread preferred by the locals of Leh Ladakh in most of their dishes. This bread is brown in colour with a thick crust baked in a pan. The bread is served with a butter tea which is prepared by adding a pinch of butter and salt. The unique taste of butter tea with a loaf of Khambir is the best thing you will ask for in Leh Ladakh. The best place to try– The Tibetan Kitchen.

3.Tigmo/Teemo Tigmo or Teemo is yet another superb cuisine you must never miss in Leh Ladakh. The best part of this dish is you can mould it as vegetarian or non-vegetarian. The sweet and spicy taste of this dish is going to add more to your taste buds. This dish is a flavoured stew and can be consumed as a snack and also as a meal. The best place to try– Lamayuru Restaurant.

4.Momos A steamed bun with a superb stuff embedded in it will make your day and eventually your vacation a memorable one. The Ladakhi Momos are definitely going to allure your taste buds and you will just hate having them in any other place. The best place to try– The Momos centres on the street side.

5.Mokthuk Mokthuk is a superb blend of momos and soup, to be peculiar. The added vegetables in the curry and some unique spices from the hills are enough to give your vacation a tasty curve. It is a delicious concoction and the dish promises a superb experience overall. The best place to try– Gesmo Restaurant.

6.skyu The preparation involves root veggies like carrot, turnip and potatoes together with soft wheat dough that is kneaded into small balls. The entire mix is cooked with meat and fresh vegetables.This is almost a daily dish for the locals of Leh Ladakh and is usually accompanied by momos or dumplings. The best place to try– Summer Harvest Restaurant.

7.Chhupri or Yak cheese Made from Yak’s milk by churning it and adding lime and other citrus fruits, this cheese is available in the local bakeries. The use of this particular cheese just adds on to the taste; for instance, a layer of this cheese on momos is the best thing ever you will taste. The best place to try– The local vendors in Leh Ladakh.

8.Paba and Tangtur Paba and Tangtur is a nutritious dish of Leh Ladakh and is said to be the staple food of Leh Ladakh. The ingredients involve peas and wheat which is very healthy and offers a sumptuous taste altogether. Tangtur is basically the buttermilk which is served along with some veggies to make this cuisine even tastier. The best place to try– All over Leh-Ladakh in small cafes.
